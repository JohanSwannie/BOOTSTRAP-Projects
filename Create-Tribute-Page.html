Sergei Nakariakov

Best Classical Trumpeter of the Modern Era
Sergei Nakariakov, in the middle, has made a significant contribution towards the experience of classical trumpeters all over the world.

Here is a timeline of Sergei Nakariakov's life:

  • 1977 - Born in Gorky, Russia.
  • 1986 - Studied the piano and was obliged to give up playing it after a car accident.
  • 1991 - Nakariakov moved with his family to Israel and made his debut at the Salzburg Festival the same year.
  • 1992 - His started his recording career when he signed an exclusive contract with Teldec Classics International and made his CD debut at the age of 15.
  • 1998 - He recorded the theme music “Urara in the Sky” for a NHK’s popular TV serial in the morning “Ten-Urara”.
  • 2001 - This was followed by his screen debut in the Japanese film “Taiga no Itteki” in September 2001, for which he also played the theme music.
  • 2004 - “J-Themes”, a CD featuring Japanese TV/film music was released by avex-CLASSICS.
